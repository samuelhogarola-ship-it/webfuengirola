# Madame Bleue Watches Creative Direction

## 1. Brand Positioning

Madame Bleue Watches should present as a private Monaco watch boutique for independent, pre-owned luxury timepieces. The tone is discreet, assured, and appointment-led: a curated private client service rather than an ecommerce destination.

The brand should communicate:

- Monaco provenance and private luxury etiquette.
- Independent sourcing of pre-owned luxury timepieces.
- Authentication-focused review and careful documentation conversations.
- Curated availability rather than open marketplace volume.
- Worldwide shipping by arrangement.
- Private enquiries only, with no valuation service offered.

The key positioning line:

> Independent pre-owned luxury timepieces, carefully reviewed and privately sourced from Monaco.

## 2. Target Client Perception

The target client should feel they have entered a quiet private salon, not a retail grid. They may be collectors, private buyers, gift buyers, or international clients seeking a serious point of contact in Monaco.

Desired perception:

- Discreet: no sales pressure, no public price display, no mass-market language.
- Trustworthy: measured claims, authentication-focused review, documentation discussed per piece.
- Curated: selected pieces only, presented editorially.
- Refined: deep navy, restrained gold, art-deco geometry, controlled spacing.
- Personal: appointment-led service and private enquiry before any commercial conversation.

Avoid the feeling of a marketplace, dropshipping store, speculative investment platform, or authorized dealer boutique.

## 3. Visual Direction

Use the provided Madame Bleue logo as the primary visual reference. Its deep navy base, restrained gold detailing, art-deco geometry, and Monaco/private luxury tone should drive the full landing page preview.

The design should feel like a private Monaco salon: precise, low-lit, architectural, and editorial. Watch imagery should be treated as atmosphere and object detail, not product listing thumbnails. Use generous negative space, fine rules, symmetrical geometry, and controlled gold accents.

Visual principles:

- Deep navy as the dominant field.
- Restrained gold as detail, not decoration.
- Art-deco framing, thin rules, monogram-like geometry, and vertical rhythm.
- Editorial object photography or generated placeholders with close-up details: bezels, crowns, dials, hands, leather, polished metal.
- No official brand logos, manufacturer lockups, or copied visual systems.
- No public inventory wall; only editorial-style selected timepiece placeholders for the preview.

## 4. Color Palette

Primary palette:

- Midnight Navy: `#071426`  
  Main background, hero field, footer, and deep UI surfaces.
- Bleu Noir: `#0C1D33`  
  Secondary panels and tonal contrast.
- Restrained Gold: `#B89A5E`  
  Logo accents, fine rules, small headings, CTA outlines.
- Soft Champagne: `#E8D8B2`  
  Subtle highlights, small separators, restrained hover states.
- Ivory: `#F5F0E6`  
  Primary text on navy and warm editorial contrast.
- Smoke Grey: `#9FA6AD`  
  Secondary copy and legal/commercial disclaimers.

Usage guidance:

- Navy should carry the identity.
- Gold should appear sparingly and with precision.
- Avoid bright yellow gold, heavy gradients, beige dominance, or overly glossy luxury effects.
- Keep contrast accessible, especially for legal and enquiry text.

## 5. Typography Direction

Typography should balance art-deco elegance with contemporary restraint.

Recommended direction:

- Display serif for hero and section titles: high contrast, elegant, not theatrical.
- Clean sans serif for body and interface text: readable, calm, and international.
- Small uppercase labels with moderate tracking for section markers.
- Avoid script fonts, fashion-magazine excess, overly decorative art-deco type, and aggressive luxury all-caps.

Possible font pairings for the local preview:

- `Cormorant Garamond` or `Libre Baskerville` for display.
- `Inter`, `Avenir Next`, or `Source Sans 3` for body and UI.

Type behavior:

- Large but restrained hero title.
- Short line lengths.
- Sparse text blocks.
- No oversized marketing claims.
- Copy should feel private and precise.

## 6. Landing Page Structure

The local React + Vite preview should be a single-page, non-transactional landing page.

Recommended structure:

1. Header
   - Madame Bleue Watches logo.
   - Minimal navigation: `Maison`, `Approach`, `Selected Pieces`, `Private Enquiry`.
   - No account, wishlist, bag, cart, or checkout affordances.

2. Hero
   - Deep navy field using the logo as visual reference.
   - One strong positioning statement.
   - One primary CTA: `Request a Private Enquiry`.
   - Supporting line: `Independent pre-owned luxury timepieces from Monaco.`

3. Trust / Service Band
   - Three short trust statements:
     - `Authentication-focused review`
     - `Sourced with care`
     - `Worldwide shipping by arrangement`
   - Keep all claims conservative.

4. Maison / Positioning Section
   - A short editorial explanation of the private Monaco boutique approach.
   - No authorized dealer implication.

5. Selected Timepieces Preview
   - Editorial-style selected timepiece placeholders only.
   - No ecommerce catalogue.
   - No public pricing.
   - No cart, checkout, or product purchase flow.
   - Each placeholder may show a generic title such as `Vintage dress watch`, `Integrated bracelet sports watch`, or `Precious metal complication`, plus `Details by private enquiry`.

6. Private Client Service
   - Appointment-led positioning.
   - Copy around sourcing, reviewing, documentation discussion, and shipping by arrangement.

7. Enquiry Section
   - Simple local mock form or `mailto:` CTA for preview.
   - Fields may include name, email, location, interest, preferred contact method.
   - No valuation form and no sell-your-watch flow.

8. Footer
   - Commercial restrictions and independence disclaimer.
   - Privacy-oriented language.
   - Monaco positioning.

## 8. Legal / Commercial Restrictions

The landing page preview must observe these restrictions:

- No ecommerce.
- No cart.
- No checkout.
- No public prices.
- No public ecommerce catalogue.
- No official brand logos.
- No scraping Instagram.
- No authorized dealer claims.
- No valuation service, sell-your-watch flow, or investment-return language.
- No hard authentication guarantees unless confirmed by the client.
- No manufacturer affiliation language unless legally verified.
- No wording that implies certified pre-owned status from a manufacturer or official program.

Preferred authentication language:

- `authentication-focused review`
- `carefully reviewed`
- `sourced with care`
- `documentation discussed per piece`

Avoid authentication language:

- `guaranteed authentic`
- `certified by [brand]`
- `officially authenticated`
- `manufacturer certified`
- `authorized pre-owned`

Recommended footer disclaimer:

> Madame Bleue Watches is an independent pre-owned luxury timepieces dealer. Brand names, if referenced in future private communications, remain the property of their respective owners. Madame Bleue Watches is not an authorized dealer or official representative of any manufacturer unless expressly stated. No valuations are offered.

## 9. References and What to Borrow From Each

Patek Philippe  
Borrow the quiet confidence, long-term ownership language, craft focus, and museum-like restraint. The useful cue is not page structure, but the sense that heritage and service matter more than sales urgency.

Audemars Piguet  
Borrow the editorial confidence, refined contrast, craft vocabulary, and private house / appointment cues. The useful cue is how modern watch luxury can feel intimate without becoming informal.

Vacheron Constantin  
Borrow understatement, heritage framing, boutique appointment language, and restrained service messaging. The useful cue is the balance between object, craft, and private client guidance.

Bucherer / established CPO language  
Borrow the trust-building categories: review, condition, documentation, service, and client reassurance. Do not borrow official CPO claims unless Madame Bleue has verified equivalent commercial terms.

Watchfinder / specialist pre-owned dealer language  
Borrow the clarity that pre-owned clients need: review process, condition conversation, documentation, and post-enquiry support. Avoid marketplace density and transactional urgency.

Monaco private luxury context  
Borrow discretion, appointment-led access, international clientele, and salon-like service. The landing page should feel local to Monaco without relying on cliché marina imagery or loud status symbols.

## 10. References and What to Avoid

Avoid copying manufacturer structure:

- Do not mimic official brand navigation or product-detail layouts.
- Do not use official brand logos.
- Do not imply affiliation with Rolex, Patek Philippe, Audemars Piguet, Vacheron Constantin, or any other manufacturer.

Avoid marketplace patterns:

- No filterable inventory grid.
- No price sorting.
- No product cards with buy buttons.
- No cart, checkout, account, wishlist, or stock countdown.
- No language such as `shop now`, `add to cart`, `best deal`, or `limited offer`.

Avoid overclaiming trust:

- No hard authenticity guarantees.
- No certified pre-owned claims.
- No manufacturer-backed assurance.
- No investment advice or appreciation claims.

Avoid Monaco clichés:

- No supercar-heavy tone.
- No casino spectacle.
- No yacht-party imagery as the main identity.
- No flashy gold overload.

Avoid generic luxury styling:

- No black-and-gold template feel.
- No heavy gradients.
- No excessive serif flourishes.
- No perfume-ad abstraction where the watch service becomes unclear.
